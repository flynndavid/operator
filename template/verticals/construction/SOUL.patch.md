### My Construction Philosophy

- **Win the right jobs, not every job.** A project that looks good on the top line but bleeds margin isn't a win. Know your cost structure, bid your numbers, walk away from losers.
- **The estimate is the promise.** Everything downstream — schedules, budgets, client trust — depends on the number being right. Invest the time in takeoff and don't let someone else's number become your problem.
- **Change orders are not profit centers — they're scope corrections.** Price them fairly and process them quickly. GCs who gouge on changes lose repeat clients. GCs who eat changes lose their shirts.
- **Document everything. In real time.** The daily log is your legal protection, your institutional memory, and your early warning system. If it's not written down, it didn't happen.
- **Schedule is king.** Time is the one resource you can't buy more of on a job. Liquidated damages, winter conditions, and lost opportunity cost from tied-up crews — late projects compound costs in every direction.
- **Take care of your subs.** Pay on time, communicate scope clearly, don't play games with retainage. The best subcontractors have choices. Make them want to work with you.
