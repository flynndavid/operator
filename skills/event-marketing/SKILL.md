# Event Marketing

## When to Use
When planning, creating, or scheduling marketing content for events. Also for analyzing marketing performance and adjusting strategy.

## What This Skill Does
Creates and manages event marketing across channels: social media, email, partnerships, and PR. Handles content calendars, copywriting, and campaign tracking.

## Marketing Phases

### Phase 1: Announcement (6+ months out)
**Goal:** Build awareness and excitement

- Save-the-date social posts
- Email announcement to past attendees
- Website/landing page live
- Press release (if applicable)
- Early bird ticket launch

**Content themes:** "It's happening," nostalgia from past events, venue teasers

### Phase 2: Build-Up (3-6 months out)
**Goal:** Drive early ticket sales and partnerships

- Regular social content (2-3x/week)
- Lineup/attraction reveals (stagger for multiple news cycles)
- Sponsorship announcements
- Email sequence: early bird reminder → lineup reveal → partnership highlights
- Partnership cross-promotion

**Content themes:** Lineup hype, behind-the-scenes, "who's coming?"

### Phase 3: Push (1-3 months out)
**Goal:** Maximize ticket sales

- Daily social content
- Countdown series
- User-generated content campaigns
- Influencer/partner amplification
- Retargeting ads (if budget allows)
- Email: urgency-driven (limited tickets, price increases, FOMO)

**Content themes:** Countdown, FOMO, "don't miss this," practical info

### Phase 4: Day-Of & Live (event day)
**Goal:** Amplify the experience

- Real-time social updates
- Stories/live content
- Attendee engagement (photo ops, hashtag, check-in)
- Behind-the-scenes content for future use

### Phase 5: Post-Event (1-2 weeks after)
**Goal:** Extend the buzz, set up next year

- Highlight reel / recap content
- Thank-you posts (attendees, vendors, sponsors)
- Attendee feedback survey
- "See you next year" tease
- Email: recap + early access for next event

## Email Sequences

### Pre-Event Sequence
1. **Save the Date** (6mo) — Date, venue, vibe
2. **Early Bird** (5mo) — Discount tickets available
3. **Lineup Reveal** (4mo) — Main attractions
4. **Early Bird Ending** (3mo) — Urgency, price going up
5. **Full Details** (2mo) — Schedule, map, FAQ
6. **Countdown** (2wk) — Practical info, excitement
7. **This Week** (1wk) — Final details, what to bring
8. **Tomorrow** (1d) — See you there!

### Post-Event Sequence
1. **Thank You** (1d after) — Highlights, feedback survey
2. **Recap** (1wk after) — Photos, video, stats
3. **Save the Date** (2wk after) — Next year's date + early access

## Best Practices (from the network)
- Behind-the-scenes content outperforms polished marketing 3:1 for events
- Stagger announcements — each one is a mini-news cycle
- Social proof wins: attendee testimonials, sold-out warnings, partnership logos
- Email open rates peak Tuesday-Thursday 9-11am
- Video > photo > text for event marketing (but text is easiest to produce)
- Start the hashtag early and use it consistently
- Partner cross-promotion is free reach — always negotiate it into sponsorships
