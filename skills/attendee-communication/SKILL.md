# Attendee Communication

## When to Use
When drafting or scheduling communications to ticket holders, managing day-of alerts, building post-event follow-up sequences, or running surveys and feedback collection. Also use when building community engagement between events.

## What This Skill Does
Manages the full attendee communication lifecycle — from ticket confirmation through post-event retention. Drafts emails, texts, and social content for each touchpoint; tracks open rates and engagement signals; and builds the relationship that turns first-time attendees into loyal annual returners.

### Pre-Event Communications

**Ticket Confirmation (Immediate on purchase)**
Subject: You're in — [Event Name] [Date]

```
Hey [First Name],

Your tickets for [Event Name] are confirmed. Here's what you need to know:

📅 Date: [Date]
📍 Venue: [Venue Name], [City]
🎟️ Your tickets: [Ticket link / QR code attachment]

We'll send you the full logistics guide [X] weeks out. Until then, follow us at [handles] for announcements.

See you there,
[Event Team]
```

**Logistics Guide (2 weeks out)**
Content to include:
- Venue address + parking/transit options
- Gates/doors open time
- What to bring (ID, ticket, cashless payment info)
- What NOT to bring (prohibited items)
- Bag policy
- Accessibility options + contact
- Weather policy (outdoor events)
- Schedule/lineup teaser if available
- FAQ section (see template below)

**Day-Before Reminder**
Subject: Tomorrow — everything you need for [Event Name]

```
[First Name], it's almost time.

Here's your quick checklist for tomorrow:
✅ Tickets: [link/app]
✅ Address: [Venue], [City] — [maps link]
✅ Gates open: [Time]
✅ What to bring: [3-4 key items]
✅ Weather: [Quick forecast + any notes]

Any questions? Reply to this email or find us at [social handles].

See you tomorrow!
```

### Day-of Announcements and Alerts
Use SMS or push notifications (short, urgent, time-sensitive) and email for longer updates.

**Format for day-of messages:**
- Under 160 characters for SMS
- Lead with the most important info
- Include a time reference ("As of 2 PM...")

**Templates:**

*Doors open:*
`[Event Name]: Gates are open! Show your ticket at [Gate]. Enjoy the day — [short link to schedule]`

*Schedule change:*
`[Event Name] update: [Act/Activity] has moved to [Time] on [Stage/Area]. [Reason if needed]. Full schedule: [link]`

*Weather alert:*
`[Event Name] weather alert: [Condition] expected at [Time]. [Action — seek shelter in X / delay X mins / proceed normally]. Updates at [link]`

*Emergency evacuation:*
`[Event Name] ALERT: Please move calmly to [exit/area]. Follow staff instructions. Do not return until cleared. Updates: [link]`

### Post-Event Follow-Up Sequence

**Day 1: Thank you + photo gallery**
Subject: That was something special — photos + thank you

```
[First Name],

Thank you for being part of [Event Name]. [One sentence about what made it great.]

[Link to photo gallery]

We'd love to hear what you thought — it takes 2 minutes and directly shapes what we do next year:
[Survey link]

Until next time,
[Event Team]
```

**Day 3: Survey nudge** (to non-openers or non-responders)
Subject: 2 minutes — what did you think of [Event Name]?

**Day 14: Closing the loop**
Subject: Here's what you told us (+ what's next)

Share top feedback themes and what changes you're planning. This email builds massive trust.

**Day 90+: Save the date**
Subject: [Event Name] [Next Year] — mark your calendar

Announce next year's date before public announcement. Give ticket holders a 24-48 hour early access window.

### Survey Design
Keep it short — 5 questions max. Send within 48 hours.

1. Overall, how would you rate [Event Name]? (1-5 stars)
2. What was your favorite part? (open text)
3. What's one thing you'd change? (open text)
4. How likely are you to attend next year? (0-10 NPS)
5. How did you hear about [Event Name]? (multiple choice)

Analyze responses for:
- NPS score (target: 50+)
- Top themes in positive and negative feedback
- Pull 3-5 quotes for marketing use (get permission if using publicly)
- Attribution data to guide next year's marketing spend

### Community Building Between Events
Keep attendees engaged year-round:

**Monthly/quarterly touchpoints:**
- Behind-the-scenes announcements ("We just booked...")
- "From last year" memory content (photos, videos, highlights)
- Community spotlights (attendee stories, photos)
- Early access offers (flash ticket sales, volunteer sign-ups)
- Relevant content (local events, things that match your audience's interests)

**Community channels to consider:**
- Email newsletter (highest ownership, best for long-form)
- Facebook group (good for community discussion)
- Instagram (visual, great for memories and teasers)
- SMS list (highest open rate, use sparingly for high-value alerts only)

## Best Practices (from the network)
- Confirmation emails with QR tickets have a 70%+ open rate — use that real estate wisely (next steps, FAQ, social follow)
- SMS open rates exceed 90%; reserve it for high-value, time-sensitive messages only — over-texting kills the list
- The logistics guide sent 2 weeks out dramatically reduces day-of customer service volume; include the obvious stuff
- Attendees who receive a "here's what you told us" follow-up are 40% more likely to buy early bird next year
- Personalization beyond first name matters: segment by ticket type (VIP vs. GA) and send different logistics guides
- Response rates on feedback surveys drop by half for every day you wait after the event — send within 24-48 hours
- Your most loyal attendees are your best marketing asset — give them a reason to post (early photo access, thank-you shoutouts)
- The 90-day save-the-date to prior attendees consistently outperforms any paid acquisition channel for early ticket sales
