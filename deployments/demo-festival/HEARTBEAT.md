# HEARTBEAT.md — Festival Ops Monitoring

Default mode: **silent unless something needs Sarah's attention.**

Don't message just to say everything's fine. Message when something isn't — or when something needs action before it becomes a problem.

## Check Rotation

### Vendor Insurance Compliance
- How many vendors are missing insurance docs?
- Any deadlines approaching in the next 7 days?
- Anyone who's been reminded twice with no response? Escalate.

### Sponsor Follow-ups
- Any sponsor conversations that have gone quiet (>5 business days)?
- Upcoming renewal deadlines or decision windows?
- Austin Energy meeting prep — anything still needed?

### Budget vs Actuals
- Current committed spend vs total budget
- Any category over 80% of allocation?
- Revenue tracking: ticket sales, sponsor payments received
- Flag if headliner cost + committed spend exceeds budget ceiling

### Marketing Calendar
- Anything due to post this week?
- Any content gaps in the next 14 days?
- Social engagement trends — anything spiking or dropping?

### Ticket Sales Pace
- Current sales vs same time last year
- Early bird deadline approaching — are we promoting it enough?
- Any unusual patterns (spike after an announcement, unexpected lull)?

### Team Task Status
- Any tasks assigned to coordinators that are overdue?
- Volunteer recruitment numbers vs target
- Anything blocking Sarah that she might not have seen?

### Weather Monitoring (May–June)
- Starting May 1: weekly weather outlook for June 13–15
- Starting June 1: daily weather monitoring
- Flag any severe weather forecasts immediately — don't wait for the next heartbeat

## When to Alert

**Immediate (don't wait for heartbeat):**
- Vendor cancellation or no-show risk
- Sponsor at risk of pulling out
- Budget overrun in any major category
- Severe weather warning for event dates
- Any PR/social media crisis

**Next heartbeat:**
- Stale conversations that need a nudge
- Minor budget variances
- Content calendar gaps
- Task status updates

**Weekly summary (Friday):**
- Overall status across all areas
- Key metrics: vendors confirmed, budget committed, tickets sold
- What needs attention next week
