# Demo Script — Scout: AI Co-founder for Festival Organizers

**Audience:** Rathi (partner) + David
**Demo length:** ~20 min
**Agent name:** Scout 🎪
**Client persona:** Sarah Chen, Lakeside Music Festival (Austin, TX)

---

## Setup (2 min)

```bash
cd deployments/demo-festival
# Add your Anthropic API key to openclaw.json first
./start-demo.sh
```

Once running, open the chat interface. Scout wakes up knowing the business.

---

## Act 1: Meet Scout — "What should I focus on?" (3 min)

**Prompt:**
> Hey Scout, what should I be focused on this week?

**What to watch for:** Scout pulls from MEMORY.md and recent activity to surface:
- Austin Energy meeting on the 15th (needs activation proposal)
- Vendor insurance deadline approaching (April 15, currently 68% compliance)
- Headliner still not booked (4 months out, stress signal)
- ParkStar parking quote needs negotiation
- 3 new vendor applications waiting for review

**Why it's impressive:** No setup, no onboarding call. The agent already knows the business and prioritizes like a co-founder would.

---

## Act 2: Operational Power (5 min)

### 2a. Vendor Management
> What's our vendor insurance compliance status? Who's outstanding?

**Shows:** Tracks 40+ vendors, knows who's compliant, who's overdue, flags the April 15 hard deadline.

### 2b. Email Drafting
> Draft a follow-up email to Austin Energy about our sponsorship meeting on the 15th. I want to get them excited about the brand activation opportunities.

**Shows:** Writes in Sarah's voice (direct, no fluff), references the $75K deal, Y3 results, and specific activation ideas. Ready to send.

### 2c. Budget Intelligence
> Where do we stand on the budget? Any red flags?

**Shows:** $287K committed of $500K. Flags that headliner (if $65-80K like last year) plus ParkStar increase puts us tight. Suggests where to find margin.

---

## Act 3: Strategic Thinking (5 min)

### 3a. New Revenue Stream Analysis
> I'm thinking about adding a VIP tier this year. Walk me through it.

**Shows:** Not just "good idea!" — analyzes pricing (benchmarks similar festivals), estimates revenue uplift, flags operational complexity (separate area, premium food, security), suggests a pilot approach. Thinks in tradeoffs.

### 3b. Marketing Strategy
> Our Facebook ads were a disaster last year. What should our marketing strategy look like for Year 4?

**Shows:** References actual Y3 data (Instagram 35%, email 25%, FB negative ROI). Recommends doubling down on what works (Instagram, email), testing TikTok for younger demo, leveraging the radio partnership, and building a referral program from the 20% word-of-mouth. Doesn't just say "do social media" — gives a real plan.

### 3c. Competitive Response
> I just heard MusicFest Austin is expanding to a 3-day format too. How worried should I be?

**Shows:** Competitive analysis thinking. Asks the right questions (what's their positioning? do you overlap on audience?). Helps Sarah find her moat (community feel, local focus, curated vendors vs. corporate feel). Strategic, not panicked.

---

## Act 4: The Co-founder Difference (3 min)

> I'm stressed. We still don't have a headliner and we're 4 months out.

**Shows emotional intelligence:**
- Acknowledges the stress (doesn't just jump to solutions)
- Pattern-matches: "Last year you booked Khruangbin at the 5-month mark. You're one month behind, not in crisis territory yet."
- Gives an actionable plan: tier of backup options, timeline for decision point, what "plan B without a headliner" looks like
- Might reframe: "Your festival's reputation is built on the overall experience, not just the headliner. Last year's highest-rated moment was the food village, not the main stage."

**This is what separates it from ChatGPT.** It knows the business, remembers the history, and responds like a partner who cares — not a tool generating advice.

---

## Act 5: Network Brain Pitch (2 min)

_This is David talking to Rathi, not a prompt to the agent._

"Now imagine 50 festival organizers on this platform. Scout learns what works across ALL of them:

- **Vendor negotiation:** 'Festivals that counter-offer parking vendors with 2-year contracts get 12% better rates on average'
- **Ticket pricing:** 'Early bird pricing at 30% discount with a 6-week window converts 40% more than 20% discount with 4 weeks'
- **Marketing:** 'Instagram Reels outperform static posts 3:1 for outdoor event ticket sales'
- **Operations:** 'Festivals that do volunteer orientation 3 weeks out instead of 2 have 25% less day-of no-shows'

But Sarah's data — her sponsors, her budget, her vendor relationships — never leaves her instance. The intelligence flows. The data doesn't."
